I am an undergraduate student at COEP Technological University, India, pursuing a B.Tech (Honors) in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering. My research focuses on applying machine learning and signal processing to both communication systems and imaging. I work on 5G/6G network self-healing, anomaly detection, jamming analysis, and visible light and optical wireless communications (LiFi). In parallel, I explore medical and digital image analysis, developing deep learning models for segmentation, detection, and steganalysis. My goal is to design intelligent, data-driven algorithms that advance reliability and efficiency in next-generation communication and imaging systems.

Previously, I have worked as a Research Intern at aeroLiFi GmbH (Germany), focusing on optical wireless and LiFi-based communication systems, and as a Volunteer Research Assistant at Stanford University with Dr. Michael Ghijsen, applying deep learning to medical image segmentation and analysis.

As an undergraduate at COEP Technological University, I have worked with Dr. Shilpa Metkar on digital forensics and steganalysis, and with Dr. Shubhangi Deshmukh on machine learning–based 5G signal anomaly detection and jamming localization.
