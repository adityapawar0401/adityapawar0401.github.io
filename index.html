I am an undergraduate student at COEP Technological University, India, pursuing a B.Tech (Honors) in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering. My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, signal processing, and communication systems, with applications in both wireless networks and imaging.

Previously, I have worked as a Research Intern at aeroLiFi GmbH (Germany), focusing on optical wireless and LiFi-based communication systems. My work involved experimental design and analysis for high-speed LiFi links, integrating them with conventional RF systems. I also served as a Volunteer Research Assistant at Stanford University with Dr. Michael Ghijsen, where I applied deep learning to medical image segmentation and tissue quantification using CT imaging.

As an undergraduate at COEP Technological University, I have worked with Dr. Shilpa Metkar on digital forensics and steganalysis, developing CNN-based models for detecting hidden information in images. In parallel, I collaborated with Dr. Shubhangi Deshmukh on machine learning–based 5G signal anomaly detection and jamming localization, contributing to robust and self-healing network mechanisms. These experiences have strengthened my interest in developing intelligent, adaptive algorithms that enhance the reliability and interpretability of next-generation communication and imaging systems.
